Technical art work often breaks down at the interface boundaries. A tool might be powerful, but if
its assumptions are hidden, every handoff costs time.

We prefer pipeline surfaces that make the next step obvious. That means opinionated presets, strong
defaults, and visual checkpoints that tell a team whether a scene is healthy before anyone opens a
profiler.

This is also why static content tooling matters for a studio site. Articles and documentation should
be easy to author without routing every change through bespoke page editing. A content collection is
more resilient than a growing pile of manually duplicated HTML.
